What is mental health?
Mental health is the state of emotional, psychological, and social well-being that influences how individuals think, feel, act, cope with stress, and relate to others. It is more than just the absence of illness, acting as a crucial foundation for functioning well at home, work, and in the community.

Key aspects of mental health include:
- Components: It combines emotional (feelings), psychological (thoughts), and social (interactions) well-being.
- Functioning: It determines how we handle stress, make choices, and realize our abilities.
- Lifelong Importance: It is crucial at every stage of life, from childhood through adulthood.
- Resilience: Good mental health allows for coping with life's, managing negative thoughts, and building strong, positive relationships.

Warning Signs of Declining Mental Health
Changes in thinking, feeling, or behaving can signal a decline, such as:
- Persistent low mood or sadness.
- Extreme mood changes.
- Withdrawal from friends and activities.
- Significant changes in sleep or diet.
- Inability to manage daily activities

How to improve mental health
Improving mental health involves a combination of physical self-care, social connection, and positive cognitive habits. Key strategies include exercising regularly, prioritizing 7–9 hours of sleep, eating nutritious meals, practicing gratitude, maintaining social connections, setting small goals, and limiting social media consumption.

Key Strategies to Improve Mental Health
- Prioritize Physical Health:
	- Exercise: Engage in at least 30 minutes of daily walking, or a mix of aerobic and resistance training to reduce stress and boost mood.
	- Sleep: Maintain a consistent sleep schedule to get 7–9 hours of quality sleep, crucial for emotional regulation.
	- Diet: Eat balanced meals and stay hydrated, which influences energy levels and brain function.
- Nurture Social Connections:
	- Stay Connected: Spend time with family and friends to build a strong support network.
	- Socialize: Share feelings with trusted individuals to reduce emotional burdens.
- Practice Mindfulness and Cognitive Techniques:
	- Mindfulness: Pay attention to the present moment, including thoughts, feelings, and the surrounding environment to lower blood pressure and improve well-being.
	- Gratitude: Actively practice gratitude by focusing on positive aspects of life.
	- Manage Negative Thoughts: Avoid irrational self-blame and practice self-acceptance during emotional highs and lows.
- Engage in Meaningful Activities:
	- Set Goals: Set and achieve small, short-term goals to build a sense of accomplishment and control.
	- Hobbies: Engage in creative arts, gardening, or reading to relax.
- Lifestyle Adjustments:
	- Limit Negative Content: Take breaks from social media and news that cause stress, anger, or anxiety.
	- Avoid Harmful Substances: Reduce or stop the use of alcohol, tobacco, and other drugs, as they can cause mental, physical, and emotional instability.
- Seek Professional Help: If feelings of sadness, anxiety, or stress become overwhelming, seek support from a mental health professional

Statistics of mental health
Over 1 billion people globally live with mental health disorders,, with 1 in 7 experiencing a mental health condition. Anxiety and depression are the most common, accounting for 1 in 6 years lived with disability. Nearly half of people in OECD countries experience a mental health problem in their lifetime, yet many go untreated.

Global and Regional Statistics
- Prevalence: Approximately 15% of the world's population experienced a mental disorder in 2023.
- Gender: Women are more likely to experience common mental health problems, such as anxiety, compared to men.
- Mortality: People with severe mental health conditions die 10 to 20 years earlier than the general population.
- Suicide: Suicide is the 11th leading cause of death in the U.S. (2023), with 90% of those who die by suicide having experienced symptoms of a mental health disorder.
